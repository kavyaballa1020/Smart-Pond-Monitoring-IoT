Smart Pond Monitoring

Smart Pond Monitoring is a project aimed at monitoring various parameters of ponds to ensure optimal conditions for aquatic life. The project utilizes three main sensors:

Temperature Sensor

Measures the temperature of pond water. A temperature sensor is a device that measures the temperature of its environment. It converts changes in temperature into an electrical signal that can be used to regulate the operation of a device.

pH Sensor

Monitors the pH level of pond water. A pH meter or pH probe is an instrument used to determine the acidity or alkalinity of a solution, typically recorded as a pH value. The pH scale ranges from 0 to 14, with values below 7 indicating acidic conditions and values above 7 indicating alkaline conditions.

Water Level Sensor

Accurately measures the water level in the pond. A water level sensor is a device that measures the height or depth of a liquid, typically used in applications such as flood control, water management, and environmental monitoring.

The quality of water available to aquatic life is crucial for their health and well-being. Our monitoring system provides real-time data on key parameters to ensure the sustainability of aquatic environments.

With our IoT-enabled monitoring system, we aim to contribute to the enhancement of aquaculture farming productivity by providing actionable insights and recommendations based on data collected from the sensors.
